#### MiFID II Unbundling Impact
[MiFID II](https://www.esma.europa.eu/policy-rules/mifid-ii-and-mifir) (January 2018) forced EU asset managers to separate payments for research from execution costs. This caused a structural decline in execution-only commission rates globally. The FCA confirmed reduced dealing commissions and research budgets in the UK, per [Oxera MiFID II Impact Report](https://www.oxera.com/insights/reports/the-impact-of-mifid-ii-research-unbundling-on-equity-markets/).
#### What Specific Funds Pay: The Pass-Through Revolution
The biggest revelation in hedge fund fee structures is the **pass-through expense model**. Traditional "2 and 20" understates true investor costs:
**Citadel (Wellington Fund)** — According to a $1 billion bond prospectus, Citadel's three largest funds (Wellington, Kensington, Kensington II) generated **$56.8 billion in gross gains** from 2021 through September 2024. Investors netted only **~$30 billion** after paying:
- **$7.5 billion in management and performance fees**
- **$17 billion in pass-through expenses** (mostly employee compensation)
That means **~47% of gross alpha was consumed by total costs**. Source: [Hedgeweek Citadel Bond Prospectus Report](https://www.hedgeweek.com/citadel-bond-prospectus-reveals-56-8bn-in-gains-since-2021/) and [Nasdaq Coverage](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/citadel-bond-offering-reveals-hedge-fund-s-massive-trading-gains).
**Renaissance Technologies (Medallion Fund)** — Charges **"5 and 44"**: a 5% management fee and 44% performance fee. The fund averages **~66% gross annual returns** but delivers **~39% net** to its employee-only investors. Source: [Hedge Fund Alpha Medallion Overview](https://www.hedgefundalpha.com/medallion-fund/), [Visual Capitalist Medallion Chart](https://www.visualcapitalist.com/the-medallion-fund/), [Quantified Strategies Analysis](https://www.quantifiedstrategies.com/medallion-fund/).
**Point72 (Steve Cohen)** — Advisory fee of **~2.85% per annum (285 basis points)** plus performance fees of 10–30% on net profits. On top of this, Point72 passes through **unlimited operating expenses** — including technology, AI tools, recruitment, and employee R&D — per [Hedge Fund DB Point72 Profile](https://www.hedgefunddb.com/) and [Hedgeweek Point72 Fee Analysis](https://www.hedgeweek.com/multi-manager-fee-structures-under-scrutiny/).
**Millennium Management** — Operates a pass-through model with **no stated cap** on expenses charged to investors. Pass-throughs include PM compensation, technology infrastructure, recruitment, legal, and even office supplies. Investors also face a **5-year redemption lock**. Sources: [Hedgeweek Millennium Fee Investigation](https://www.hedgeweek.com/multi-manager-fee-structures-under-scrutiny/), [eFinancialCareers Millennium Analysis](https://www.efinancialcareers.com/news/millennium-management-fees-pass-through-expenses).

### 4. Crossing Spread Loss
The cost of failing to fully execute in dark pools / crossing networks, forcing leftover orders to leak to lit markets at worse prices.
#### Mechanics
- Trades in dark pools execute at the **NBBO midpoint**, saving half the spread vs. lit exchanges
- Major dark pools: **Liquidnet**, **Goldman Sachs Sigma X**, **Morgan Stanley InternalCross**, **Virtu Matchit** (formerly ITG POSIT)
- Typical dark pool fill rates: **5–20% of order size** — unfilled portions leak to lit markets
#### Quantified Costs
- Increasing large-in-scale dark executions reduces **implementation shortfall by 5–10 bps** vs. full lit execution — [The Trade News Dark Impact Study](https://www.thetradenews.com/dark-pool-impact-study/)
- But partial fills leaking to lit markets add **10–30 bps** in adverse selection on the unfilled portion
- **Stale pricing risk** and **latency arbitrage** in dark pools documented by [BIS Market Microstructure Report](https://www.bis.org/publ/mktc07.htm)
#### Norges Bank (World's Largest Fund) — Transaction Cost Breakdown
Norway's Government Pension Fund Global (GPFG) — **$1.8 trillion AUM**, 71.4% in equities — provides the most transparent institutional trading cost data in the world:
| Component | Cost (bps) | Dollar Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Direct costs** (commissions + taxes) | **0.42 bps** | ~$500 million | On ~$1.2 trillion equity portfolio |
| **Indirect costs** (market impact + slippage) | **1.19 bps** | ~$1.43 billion | Price movement from trade initiation to execution |
| **Total equity trading costs** | **1.61 bps** | **~$1.93 billion** | Among lowest globally for this size |
Source: [Global Trading NBIM 2024 Report](https://www.globaltrading.net/norges-bank-investment-management-2024-transaction-costs/) and [NBIM Official Reports](https://www.nbim.no/en/publications/reports/).
For comparison, **CalSTRS** (California State Teachers' Retirement System) reported **43.8 bps total investment cost in 2024** — lower than the **50.4 bps global peer average** and **59.7 bps U.S. public pension average**. CalSTRS co-investments saved **$276.2 million** in fees. Source: [PitchBook CalSTRS Cost Analysis](https://pitchbook.com/news/articles/calstrs-investment-costs-2024).

### 5. Market Impact
Market impact is the price change caused by the trade itself. For large institutional orders, it is typically the **single largest transaction cost component**.
#### The Square-Root Law I(Q) ∝ σ × √(Q/V)
The empirically proven model: market impact scales with the **square root of trade size relative to daily volume**, multiplied by daily volatility. Doubling trade size increases impact by only ~41%. Sources:
- [Kyle's Lambda — Original 1985 Microstructure Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyle%27s_lambda)
- [Bouchaud et al. Square-Root Impact Research (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/0903.2428)
- [Almgren-Chriss Optimal Execution Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implementation_shortfall) — Foundation for all modern institutional execution algorithms
#### What It Actually Costs
| Scenario | Market Impact | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Large-cap, <1% of ADV | **5–15 bps** | AQR research + industry consensus |
| Large-cap, 1–5% of ADV | **15–50 bps** | |
| Small-cap, >10% of ADV | **100–300+ bps** | |
| Norges Bank GPFG (giant fund) | **1.19 bps** | [NBIM 2024 Report](https://www.nbim.no/en/publications/reports/) |
| AQR ($1.7T trades analyzed) | **Order of magnitude smaller** than academic estimates | [AQR Paper](https://www.aqr.com/insights/research/journal-article/trading-costs) |
#### The Capacity Constraint Problem
Academic research (Berk & Green, 2004) proves that hedge fund strategies face **diminishing returns to scale**. As AUM grows:
- Market impact increases → costs eat more alpha
- Signal edges get arbitraged away → alpha decays
- Strategy capacity is finite — a fund earning 20% on $500M may earn only 5% on $5B
This is why **Renaissance's Medallion Fund caps investor capital at ~$10 billion** and returns profits annually — to avoid degrading the strategy's edge. Without leverage, Medallion generates only **modest single-digit returns with very low volatility** — leverage (12.5–17x) amplifies this into 66%+ gross returns. Sources: [Institutional Investor Medallion](https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/b1ksp4gq2p41k0/Renaissance-s-Medallion-Fund-Surged-76-in-2020-Jim-Simons-s-Firm-Didn-t-All-Benefit), [SSRN Hedge Fund Capacity Papers](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1097584).
#### Mitigation Strategies Used by Top Funds
1. **VWAP/TWAP Algorithms** — Time-weighted execution matching market patterns
2. **Dark Pool Routing** — [Liquidnet](https://www.liquidnet.com/) block crossings
3. **Almgren-Chriss Optimization** — Mathematically optimal liquidation trajectories
4. **Dimensional Fund Advisors (DFA)** — Pioneered "execution alpha" by designing strategies to exclude illiquid names and spread turnover across thousands of securities. DFA research found that **venue selection** produces statistically similar costs across lit and dark venues when accounting for all factors. Source: [Dimensional Trading Research](https://www.dimensional.com/us-en/insights/trading-insights).

### 6. Price Move Due to Your Size (Permanent Impact)
The lasting shift in an asset's fair value from trading a position large enough to signal information to the market.
#### Theoretical Framework
| Impact Type | Description | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Temporary** | Supply/demand imbalance; reverses post-trade | Minutes to hours |
| **Permanent** | Market infers information from the trade; price stays shifted | Indefinite |
Permanent impact is the **binding constraint on strategy capacity** — the reason alpha signals profitable at $500M become unprofitable at $5B.

| Goldman Sachs liquidation | **$10+ billion** sold in single session | |
