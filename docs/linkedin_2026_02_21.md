After the Feb 17 post, I implemented the three improvements I listed:

Rolling quarterly pair re-selection. Macro regime detection using VIX and sector dispersion. Walk-forward validation across 19 windows.

Also added a 38-feature signal quality gate on top of the Transformer Encoder. Features include spread z-score, momentum, RSI, pair correlation, VIX regime, and sector divergence. A normalizer is fitted on training-period data. Three thresholds must all pass before a trade is allowed.

The backtest came back at 499.6%. Sharpe 4.53.

Too clean. Same pattern as before. Investigated.

— — —

■ New Bias — Rolling Pair Look-Ahead

The rolling pair schedule stored spread histories using the full 2020–2025 dataset. Without a date restriction, the backtest loop included 2020–2022 dates. For those dates, the system defaulted to pairs validated on 2021–2023 data — then "tested" those same pairs on 2020–2022.

It knew which pairs would cointegrate in the future. Again.

Fix: restrict the backtest to 2023-01-01 onward. One line. The parameter already existed in the engine. It just wasn't being called.

Result after fix: 0.41%. 327 trades across 751 days. Barely firing.

— — —

■ Second Problem — Signal Gate Calibrated on the Wrong Regime

The signal quality gate uses a normalizer fitted on training-period spread data.

It was fitted on 2020–2022 only. Zero-rate dynamics and post-hike dynamics are structurally different. Wider z-scores. Faster mean reversion. Different sector divergence patterns. After normalization by the ZIRP-fitted scaler, post-hike features look anomalous. They fail the quality threshold and get rejected. The gate sat out nearly every day in 2023–2025.

It is a distribution shift problem. The feature normalizer was calibrated on one market regime and deployed in another.

Fix: extend calibration to include 2023-H1. Gate now recognizes both ZIRP and post-hike dynamics.

Result: 29.33%, Sharpe 1.01, 4,982 trades. 71× improvement over 0.41%.

— — —

■ Final Improvement — Hard Stop on Broken Regimes

Walk-Forward Window 16 (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025): 54% of days were reduced-scale. The system kept trading. Lost 6.52% on 438 trades.

Scaling positions to 60% is not enough when the regime is broken for three months straight. You are just losing slower.

New rule: if more than 20% of the trailing 63 days were reduced-scale, skip the day entirely. No new entries.

Also lowered the "Walking on Ice" detection threshold and tightened the scale from 60% to 40%.

Final result (v11):

→ 29.29% total return (2023-H2 to 2025)
→ Sharpe 1.63 (was 1.01)
→ Max drawdown -7.97% (was -12.44%)
→ 3,006 trades (was 4,982)

Same return. 40% fewer trades. 36% less drawdown. 61% better Sharpe.
