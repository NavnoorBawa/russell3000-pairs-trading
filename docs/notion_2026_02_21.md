## TL;DR

Added a 38-feature signal quality gate, regime detection, and rolling pair re-selection on top of the Feb 17 baseline. New complexity introduced a new look-ahead bias — v8 showed 499.6% which was fake. After eliminating it and fixing the signal gate's feature distribution mismatch: **+29.3% total return, 1.63 Sharpe, -7.97% max drawdown** over 2023–2025. Walk-forward OOS average is +0.89%/quarter. Fund comparison run on same signals under 5 institutional profiles shows only one survives: Buy-Side Institutional at +6.20%. Every leveraged profile hits its kill-switch.

## What Changed Since Feb 17

The 3.97% honest baseline from Feb 17 had three improvement items listed. All three were implemented:

| Item | Implementation |
| ---- | -------------- |
| Quarterly pair re-selection | Rolling 3-year train window, quarterly pair refresh |
| Macro regime features | VIX + 63-day cumulative sector log-return dispersion z-score |
| Walk-forward validation | 19 quarterly windows, IS→OOS era split |

Also added:

- **38-feature signal quality gate** on top of the Transformer Encoder — extracts z-score, momentum, RSI, pair quality, VIX, and sector regime features; a RobustScaler is fitted on training-period spreads to normalise feature distributions; three thresholds must all pass (z-score > 1.5, signal strength > 0.60, feature quality score > 0.65) before a trade is allowed
- **Adaptive 6-month cointegration re-test** — pairs passing 2yr ADF also re-tested on last 6 months; dropped if p > 0.15 or structural break detected
- **Regime gate** — per-day position scaling based on VIX and sector dispersion

## New Bias Found: Rolling Pair Look-Ahead

**Symptom:** v8 reported 499.6% return, Sharpe 4.53. Too good — investigated.

**Root cause:** The rolling pair schedule stored spread histories using the full 2020–2025 dataset. Without a `date_range` restriction, the backtest loop included 2020–2022 dates. For those pre-2023 dates, the system defaulted to the Q1 2023 pair selection — pairs validated using 2021–2023 data — then "tested" on 2020–2022. Classic look-ahead: the system knew which pairs would cointegrate in the future.

**Fix:** Pass `date_range=('2023-01-01', end)` to restrict `all_dates` to the true OOS period only. The `date_range` parameter already existed in the engine — it just wasn't being called.

**v9 result after fix:** 0.41% — the real unbiased number. But only 327 trades across 751 days. Barely firing.

## Second Problem Found: Signal Gate Miscalibrated on Wrong Regime

**Symptom:** v9 generated only 327 trades over 751 days (vs 3,092 in v8). Barely firing.

**Root cause:** The RobustScaler in the signal quality gate was fitted exclusively on 2020–2022 ZIRP-era spread data. The scaler learns the median and IQR of 38 features during that period. Post-hike 2023–2025 spreads have structurally different dynamics — wider z-scores, faster mean reversion, different sector divergence patterns. After normalisation by the ZIRP-fitted scaler, post-hike features look anomalous. They fail the feature quality threshold (score < 0.65) and get rejected. The gate sat out nearly every day. This is a distribution shift problem — the feature normaliser was calibrated on one market regime and deployed in another.

**Fix:** Extend scaler fitting to include 2023-H1 (2020-01-01 → 2023-06-30). Main backtest starts 2023-07-01. The gate now has a feature distribution that includes both ZIRP and post-hike dynamics.

**v10 result:** 29.33%, Sharpe 1.01, MaxDD -12.44%, 4,982 trades — 71× improvement.

## Regime Gate Tightening (v11)

v10 Walk-Forward Window 16 (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025): 54% of days were reduced-scale, yet the system still executed 438 trades and lost 6.52%.

**Problem:** Scaling positions to 60% isn't enough when the regime is broken for months straight. You're just losing slower.

**Three fixes applied:**

1. **Hard stop** — if >20% of trailing 63 days were reduced-scale → skip day entirely, no new entries
2. **WoI threshold lowered** — `cum_disp_z > 1.2` → `cum_disp_z > 0.8` (earlier regime detection)
3. **WoI scale tightened** — 60% → 40% (deeper reduction when gate fires)

## Honest Interpretation

The 29.3% main backtest return is technically unbiased — no look-ahead, correct training cutoff, proper date restrictions. But the walk-forward OOS (which is a stricter test) averages +0.89%/quarter. That's 3.6%/year.

The gap exists because the main backtest covers 2023-H2 to 2025 with a full 3.5-year scaler fitting period, while the walk-forward W10–W19 OOS windows cover the same period with *shorter* fitting windows per window. More calibration data = better signal gate performance. In production you'd have more data — so 29.3% is probably the optimistic bound and 3.6%/year is the pessimistic bound.

**The real performance is somewhere in between. Probably closer to 10–12% annualised if the 2020–2022 regime doesn't return.**

### What This Shows

**Only one real-world profile survives: Buy-Side Institutional at +6.20%.** The other four hit their kill-switch and stop trading.

**Quant HF (5–7x leverage):** Leverage is the killer. 34.88% of capital consumed by costs on 3,006 trades. Every losing day gets amplified. Kill-switch triggers Feb 2024 at -26% drawdown. Renaissance runs 12–17x leverage because their gross edge is enormous (~66%/yr). This system's gross edge is not.

**Multi-Strat Pod Shop (4x):** 13% cost drag + leverage interaction. Citadel Wellington generated $56.8B in gross gains 2021–2024 but investors only saw ~$30B after $7.5B fees and $17B pass-through expenses. 47% of gross alpha consumed. Our system doesn't generate enough gross alpha to survive that cost structure.

**Fundamental L/S HF:** High-touch commission (5 bp) + wide bid-ask (10 bp) = 14.83% cost drag on 3,006 trades. Stopped Sep 2023 — three months in.

**Retail (1x):** 22% of capital consumed by costs. IB retail at 7 bp commission, 15 bp bid-ask for mid-cap Russell 3000 names. No leverage, but costs destroy everything.

**Institutional (Norges Bank costs, 1x):** Lowest execution costs on earth — 0.42 bp commission, 1.19 bp indirect, 1.61 bp total (Norges Bank 2024 actual). No leverage. Patient execution. Survives. Returns +6.20%.

### The Cost Problem in the Main Backtest

The v11 prime fund model shows 29.29% because it uses ~0.8 bp bid-ask and ~0.1 bp commission after volume discounts — **cheaper than Norges Bank**. That's not realistic for a Russell 3000 mid-cap universe. Norges Bank trades only the most liquid large-caps on earth and still pays 1.61 bp total. Mid-cap equities have bid-ask spreads of 3–10 bp (CFA Institute TCA).

The honest realistic range:

- **Institutional costs (1.61 bp, no leverage) — already simulated:** +6.20%
- **Realistic mid-cap costs (3–4 bp, no leverage):** ~+2–4% estimated
- **Quant HF costs + leverage — already simulated:** -12.24%, stopped

The signal has a real but thin statistical edge. The internal cost model flatters it.

## What Still Needs to Improve

1. **Cost recalibration** — prime fund model (~1 bp) is too cheap for Russell 3000 mid-caps. Fund comparison shows realistic answer is +2–4% annualised at real-world costs
2. **OOS performance gap** — walk-forward OOS +0.89%/qtr vs main backtest +11.7% annualised. Regime adaptation is the real fix needed
3. **Late 2024–2025 breakdown** — W15–W19 all negative or near-zero. Mag 7 concentration, VIX suppression, or pair universe degradation — unclear which
4. **Win rate** — 52.96% barely above breakeven. Signal quality needs improvement, not quantity
5. **Profit factor 1.22** — losers almost as large as winners. Need asymmetric outcomes
