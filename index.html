02 — Methodology

System architecture

A sequential pipeline from pair selection through signal generation, regime filtering, and portfolio accounting. Each stage is independently testable and documented across 25 versions.

pair selection
Engle-Granger cointegration on all ~45,150 pairs. ADF on OLS residuals, both directions, p < 0.05. Half-life filter 4–25 days. PCA strips 5 systematic factors (~58% variance); idiosyncratic residual cointegration adds a quality bonus. CUSUM and Hurst pre-filters. Quarterly re-selection.
spread construction
1-D Kalman filter estimates a time-varying hedge ratio βt. Spread = log P₁ − βt · log P₂. Beta-weighted P&L: Δlog P₁ − βt · Δlog P₂, allocation split total/(1+|β|) per leg.
signal rule
Rolling z-score with half-life-adaptive lookback. Entry |z| > 1.8, exit |z| < 0.5. Dynamic maximum hold = clamp(2.5 × half-life, 10–25 days).
regime gate
VIX bands: >30 → 0.5×, >40 → 0.25×. 63-day cumulative sector dispersion gate — low VIX with high dispersion → 0.4×. Hard skip when >20% of trailing 63 days were reduced-scale.
validation
19 walk-forward windows, 252-day train / 63-day test, rolling by one test window. Models retrained from scratch each window. No leakage between train and test periods.

04 — Controlled ablation

ML contribution: measured at +0.02pp/qtr

A transformer-based signal-quality scorer was trained on entry-outcome labels from each training slice and used for opportunity ranking. A controlled ablation — identical data, identical code, one environment flag — quantifies its contribution. The BCE loss converges to base-rate entropy in every run: 82–91% of |z| > 1.8 entries revert within the horizon regardless of features. The entry rule has already extracted the learnable signal.

06 — Disclosure

Honest assessment

Negative results are reported in full.

What the data shows
The system generates positive gross OOS returns (+1.30%/qtr, 17/19 windows profitable). It does not generate a deployable net edge. All five institutional cost profiles — quant HF (~5–7× leverage), pod shop (~4×), fundamental L/S (~1.5–2×), unlevered institutional, and retail — produce negative net returns on the five-year backtest. IS→OOS degradation of 75% is substantially above the 30–50% range typical of well-validated strategies; whether that reflects regime break, overfit, or both is not settled by this work. The ML component was built, trained, and measured: its contribution is +0.02pp/qtr. Claims in this document are derived from logged, reproducible runs.
